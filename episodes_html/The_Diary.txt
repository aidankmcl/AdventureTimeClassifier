</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The episode begins with a game screen, which is shown to be played by <a href="/wiki/T.V." title="T.V.">T.V.</a> on a computer at <a href="/wiki/Lady_Rainicorn%27s_house" title="Lady Rainicorn's house">Lady Rainicorn's house</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lady_Rainicorn" title="Lady Rainicorn">Lady Rainicorn</a> is worried about T.V's computer gaming addiction, as <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> is seen behind reading a newspaper. Jake consoles Lady Rainicorn's worries and then asks T.V to get him a chocolate bar. T.V reluctantly agrees and goes out to get it.
</p><p>While on his way, <a href="/wiki/Pizza_People" title="Pizza People">an old man</a> greets him, but T.V. teleports away and is found near the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kingdom" title="Candy Kingdom">Candy Kingdom</a>. He goes under the <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_Bridge" title="Chocolate Bridge">Chocolate Bridge</a> and pulls out a chocolate bar from underneath. As he pulls it out, he falls in a nearby chocolate puddle. He discovers a diary in the puddle wrapped in a plastic bag, which he takes and goes out of the bridge.
</p><p>He sits on the nearby ground and opens the diary to read it. He discovers this diary to be of some unknown person named <a href="/wiki/Nurse_Pound_Cake" title="Nurse Pound Cake">B.P</a>. The diary appears to be of a typical person, so he advances to read further.
</p><p>In the diary, the author writes about her life and other things related to it. T.V. travels around different areas of <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Ooo" title="Land of Ooo">Ooo</a> while reading this diary. The diary reveals that the author wore braces, used to write songs which were not to good, or whether she will find a 'cool' guy.
</p><p>Now, the diary focuses more on guys, but still is based on the life of the author. She reveals that Taffi and Mochi, the schoolmates of the author are the most popular in the school. She then hints that she is jealous of them both, and takes it too seriously. Now, it then reveals the crush of the author, who is named Justin. She says that Justin had asked him out on the semi-formal, on which T.V. gets more interested.
</p><p>The diary now focuses more about Justin and the author. T.V. reaches Lady Rainicorn's house by night, but sits outside and keeps reading the diary. At this moment, it reveals that the author and her boyfriend had now kissed by now. T.V. then sits on his room's desk, and keeps reading the diary. The author's birthday was nearing, but she didn't want to celebrate and just scream. Justin had asked the author to meet him at <a href="/wiki/Love_Tree_Point" title="Love Tree Point">Love Tree Point</a>, saying he wanted to give her a present. After some pages, the author says that she should 'tell him', but after the page, it is torn up and T.V. gets frustrated.
</p><p>The next morning, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Rainicorn" title="Lady Rainicorn">Lady Rainicorn</a> was preparing breakfast for her family and asks T.V. to come over and have breakfast with her and Jake. T.V. says he isn't hungry, grabs an apple, and goes out. T.V. again goes out to the chocolate bridge, saying he has to get into the mind of this kid. He then starts to role act as if he were B.P. Suddenly, his imagination takes over. He is riding a roller coaster and falls in a chocolate moat. He acts as B.P in his imagination
</p><p>After this, Taffy and Mochi come there and start to talk about the CD Mochi has bought. Suddenly, Justin comes from under T.V. and repeats the same phrase the diary told him, which was to meet at Love Tree Point. T.V. again gets frustrated.
</p><p>In the house of Lady Rainicorn, Jake and Lady are playing a game of Canasta. When Jake wins the game, he says that Lady is off her game, saying he has never won a canasta. Lady then says that she is worried about T.V. because spends more time outside. Lady then says to see on T.V. to which he agrees and goes out. As Jake finds T.V., he sees that his son is in his own imagination, rolling in the puddle. As Jake wakes him up, T.V. starts to call him as his mom, to which Jake says that he is close enough. T.V. says to Jake that he needs to 'get out of his room.'
</p><p>Jake notices the diary in T.V.'s hand and snatches it away from him and also starts to read it. He then says since he was so obsessed with it, he had confused his reality with that of the diary's writer. As he notices the ripped pages, he gets interested in the mystery. T.V. points out the handwriting had changed right before the ripped off pages. Jake starts to say his theory of the pages and T.V. again goes off to his imagination.
</p><p>He says that B.P must have got emotionally distraught and stressed, that she got infected by some disease and had to be taken to the hospital. After that, he says that she must have survived, except her hand was replaced by a crab hand, which would have affected the handwriting. After this, T.V. continues the story saying that he refused to meet all visitors, which Jake gets confused saying that 'he' didn't meet the visitors. T.V. continues that he refused to see especially Justin. Justin sent him flowers, but one day they stopped. On the birthday, he decided he was going to keep his promise and goes to Love Tree Point. To his surprise, he finds Justin making out with Taffy and Mochi.
</p><p>When T.V. said Love Tree Point, Jake mentions that that's where the people went for making out on previous days. They go to the Love Tree Point, which was very old and withering away. Jake points out to tree, saying that's how this place got its name. T.V. mentions that Justin had a present around this place, and says it must be in that tree. They find the present in the tree, which is a music box. They both get emotionally stressed and start to cry, and Lady Rainicorn is seen seeing them from behind.
</p><p>The next day, Jake and T.V. make their way to the market to find out who had bought the music box. They went inside one shop, and the shop owner recognizes the box as one of her own. But she then says that it was a long time ago, and she must have made many boxes since then, so she doesn't remember its owner. As Jake makes his way out, she suddenly says that she remembers the owner of the box, describing the person as a handsome young man. She then says that it was for a girl named <a href="/wiki/Nurse_Pound_Cake" title="Nurse Pound Cake">Betsy</a>, who ran off to a cave because of the stress she had to endure, never to be seen again.
</p><p>The owner tells them in which cave she resides, and they make their way to the cave with the box. In the cave, there was a house. The house contained many things which suggested that she has practiced medical ability and mastered it. <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> finds a letter named to Justin. They read it and <a href="/wiki/Nurse_Pound_Cake" title="Nurse Pound Cake">Betsy</a> says to Justin in it that she had been good after all this stress, and says that she had figured out a cure for her crab hand. It then says that it would have been never been done between them, and signs off the letter as <a href="/wiki/Nurse_Pound_Cake" title="Nurse Pound Cake">Nurse Betsy Pound Cake</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> and <a href="/wiki/T.V." title="T.V.">T.V.</a> are then seen seeing <a href="/wiki/Nurse_Pound_Cake" title="Nurse Pound Cake">Nurse Pound Cake</a> and are happy they have solved the case. <a href="/wiki/T.V." title="T.V.">T.V.</a> asks Jake about the ripped pages, which Jake says he had found in the cave, which were mean stuff about Justin. Jake even says that it must be sad for him.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> then says they should find Justin, but <a href="/wiki/T.V." title="T.V.">T.V.</a> says no.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>The game T.V. plays at the beginning contains sound effects from the 1981 arcade game <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donkey_Kong_(video_game)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Donkey Kong (video game)">Donkey Kong.</a></i>
</li><li>The game T.V. plays is also a reference to the 1986 Role-playing game <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dragon_Quest_(video_game)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Dragon Quest (video game)">Dragon Warrior</a>.
</li><li> The post-it note reading "FeLiNa" in Betsy's abandoned shack is a reference to <a  class="text" href="http://breakingbad.wikia.com/wiki/Felina#Trivia">Breaking Bad</a>.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li>The beady-eyed cards that were first seen in "<a href="/wiki/All_the_Little_People" title="All the Little People">All the Little People</a>" are seen again. Jake and Lady play the card game <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canasta" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Canasta">Canasta</a> with them.
</li><li>The headline of the newspaper Jake reads states how the castle is wrecked, candy people are to blame. This is referring to the events of the episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Pajama_War" title="The Pajama War">The Pajama War</a>."
</li><li>The Shop Village from "<a href="/wiki/Blade_of_Grass" title="Blade of Grass">Blade of Grass</a>" is seen again in this episode.
</li><li> Butterscotch Lake, which first appeared in "<a href="/wiki/Root_Beer_Guy" title="Root Beer Guy">Root Beer Guy</a>," is seen again.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_notes">Production notes</span></h3>
<ul><li> This episode aired at 7 P.M. EST due to the season premiere of <i>Ninjago: Masters of Spinjitzu</i> airing at 7:30 p.m., the more recent time slot for <i>Adventure Time</i>.
</li></ul>
