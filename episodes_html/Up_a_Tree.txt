<p>"<b>Up a Tree</b>" is the fourth episode in the fifth season of <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>. It is the one hundred&#160;and&#160;eighth episode overall.

<nav id="toc" class="toc"><div id="toctitle"><h2>Contents</h2><span class="toctoggle">[<a href="#" class="internal" id="togglelink" data-show="show" data-hide="hide">show</a>]</span></div><ol></ol></nav>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span></h2>
<p>Finn goes on a bizarre adventure up a giant tree to retrieve a throwing disk he and Jake were playing with.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The episode starts off with <a href="/wiki/Lady_Rainicorn" title="Lady Rainicorn">Lady Rainicorn</a> walking in the <a href="/wiki/Grass_Lands" title="Grass Lands">Grass Lands</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a>. As they are walking, Lady Rainicorn comments that the wind and weather are perfect, taking the beautiful day in. After a few paces, Jake sets down a picnic blanket, which Lady Rainicorn (now noticeably pregnant with Jake's puppies) rests on. Finn then digs through the picnic basket, bringing out food as both Finn and Jake exchange ooh's and aah's. As they start to take things out of the basket, Finn finds a Frisbee, which he refers to as a "throwing-and-catching disc." He and Jake interact as if he were a regular boy with his regular dog. When Finn does his "perfect throw," the wind carries it to the top of a tall tree. Jake offers to grab the disc for Finn, but he refuses and tries to get it himself.
</p><p>Finn tries to climb up the tree with his bare hands but fails and meets a <a href="/wiki/Porcupine" title="Porcupine" class="mw-redirect">porcupine</a>. It offers for Finn to sit on him, telling him the shock would cause him to jump up to the top. Finn refuses and the porcupine does it by force. Although the porcupine's plan failed, Finn takes out the needles stuck in him and uses them to climb up the tree. He gets tired and rests on a branch. A <a href="/wiki/Squirrel_(Up_a_Tree)" title="Squirrel (Up a Tree)">squirrel</a> then offers him an apple to energize Finn. Finn refuses, but the squirrel stuffs in the apple by force. The apple was apparently cursed and shrinks Finn.
</p><p>Finn continues to climb up the tree with the porcupine needles, but they eventually break, so he takes another break. Jake <a href="/wiki/Stretchy_Powers" title="Stretchy Powers">stretches</a> up to him and notices that Finn has shrunk. He tells him that he's going to walk Lady Rainicorn home. After Jake leaves, Finn encounters the squirrel again and follows him into the tree after being ignored. He then hears the whispers of the animals. He sneaks in to see the animals discussing how to get rid of him and a large pile of objects with his Frisbee on top. In shock, Finn shouts, revealing himself. The animals then knock him out and put him in a jail cell in the tree.
</p><p>Finn wakes up in the cell with the squirrel guarding the cage. The squirrel talks about the good and bad of living in the tree and living with the rules. He then says that he wishes to fly away but the other animals say he is not a flying squirrel, and that he would run away if only he had "a buddy to break out with." Finn easily escapes through the cell bars and teams up with the squirrel to escape. The squirrel lowers Finn to his Frisbee as the animals chase them out of the tree and onto a limb. Finn throws the disc planning to ride on it, but it flies upward again. As the animals catch up, he pushes the squirrel off the branch and jumps on his back. As they are falling it is revealed that he is a regular squirrel and cannot fly. Just before they reach the ground, the Frisbee flies underneath them, catching them. On the disc, they ride across the Grasslands, past Lady's house, where Jake is making food for Lady. Finn calls out Jake's name, and he watches Finn and the squirrel riding on the Frisbee. Jake says that it's a "perfect throw" as the Frisbee flies into the sunset.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>The animals' chant, "In the tree, part of the tree," is a possible reference to the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean_(film_series)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Pirates of the Caribbean (film series)">Pirates of the Caribbean</a></i> series, in which the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flying_Dutchman_(Pirates_of_the_Caribbean)%22" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Flying Dutchman (Pirates of the Caribbean)&quot;">Flying Dutchman</a> crew chant "Part of the crew, part of the ship."
</li><li>When Finn eats a cursed apple to shrink him, it is a close reference to <i><a href="http://aliceinwonderland.wikia.com/wiki/Alice%27s_Adventures_in_Wonderland" class="extiw" title="w:c:aliceinwonderland:Alice's Adventures in Wonderland">Alice in Wonderland's</a></i> <a href="http://aliceinwonderland.wikia.com/wiki/Drink_Me_Potion" class="extiw" title="w:c:aliceinwonderland:Drink Me Potion">'Drink Me' Potion</a> and the episode of <i><a href="http://gravityfalls.wikia.com/wiki/" class="extiw" title="w:c:gravityfalls">Gravity Falls</a></i>, <a href="http://gravityfalls.wikia.com/wiki/Dreamscaperers" class="extiw" title="w:c:gravityfalls:Dreamscaperers">Dreamscaperers</a>.
</li><li>Near the end of the episode, Jake is seen stirring a bowl of pickles and ice cream at Lady's house. This is likely a reference to the fact that women sometimes develop cravings for unusual foods during their pregnancies.
</li><li>Finn and Jake throwing a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flying_disc" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Flying disc">Frisbee</a> to each other is a reference to how modern dogs and their masters play with each other.
</li><li>In the ending, Finn and the Squirrel are flying on a Frisbee when Finn waves to the Snail. Then in the next scene, Finn sees Jake in Lady's house and yells, "Hey, there's Jake. Jake!" and waves. This may be a reference to the film <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_NeverEnding_Story_(film)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:The NeverEnding Story (film)">The NeverEnding Story</a></i>. When <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_The_Neverending_Story_characters#Bastian_Balthazar_Bux" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:List of The Neverending Story characters">Bastian</a> is flying on <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_The_Neverending_Story_characters#Falkor_the_Luckdragon" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:List of The Neverending Story characters">Falkor</a> he yells "<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_The_Neverending_Story_characters#Atreyu" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:List of The Neverending Story characters">Atreyu</a>, Artax!" and waves to Atreyu who's riding his horse in the field.
</li><li>An episode of <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Garfield_Show" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:The Garfield Show">The Garfield Show</a></i> shares the same title as this episode.
</li><li>An episode of <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animaniacs" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Animaniacs">Animaniacs</a></i> shares the same title as this episode. Coincidentally, Jim Cummings lent his voice to both cartoons.
</li><li>When Finn says to the chipmunk "I'm not a chipmunk, I'm a human boy.", it is a reference to The Sword in The Stone.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Lady_Rainicorn" title="Lady Rainicorn">Lady Rainicorn</a>'s baby bump can be seen, just as it was in "<a href="/wiki/Five_More_Short_Graybles" title="Five More Short Graybles">Five More Short Graybles</a>." Her pregnancy was first revealed in "<a href="/wiki/Lady_%26_Peebles" title="Lady &amp; Peebles">Lady &amp; Peebles</a>."
</li><li>The way Finn screams when the porcupine jumps on him is the same way he screams in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Burning_Low" title="Burning Low">Burning Low</a>."
</li><li>Finn was interacting with the <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">Snail</a>, as seen when he waved back as he flew through the air. Finn had previously interacted with the snail in "<a href="/wiki/The_Lich_(episode)" title="The Lich (episode)">The Lich</a>."
<ul><li>At the same time, it is confirmed that Snail is no longer possessed by <a href="/wiki/The_Lich_(character)" title="The Lich (character)">the Lich</a>, as shown in "The Lich."
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span></h3>
<ul><li>When Jake and Lady Rainicorn left the area where they were having a picnic, they left the basket. However, in the next shot, when Finn is climbing the tree, it disappears, though it can be implied that one of them remembered the basket and got it while they were off-camera.
</li><li>The size of the porcupine needles changed when Finn shrunk.
</li><li>When the animals start chanting, the porcupine and a squirrel change places.
</li><li>When Finn and the Squirrel jumped onto the disc, they did not spin with it, which could indicate the disc was not spinning. This is physically impossible for a throwing disc to fly without spinning.
</li><li>Finn's hat appears to be bigger in some scenes.
</li><li> In some parts when Finn gets shrunk, the needles seem to shrink with him.
</li></ul>
