</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The episode opens at the <a href="/wiki/Ice_Kingdom" title="Ice Kingdom">Ice Kingdom</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> boards a bus with <a href="/wiki/Abracadaniel" title="Abracadaniel">Abracadaniel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_Dude_(character)" title="Little Dude (character)">Little Dude</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Sleeping_Magi_of_Life_Giving" title="Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving">Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beau" title="Beau">Beau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leaf_Man" title="Leaf Man">Leaf Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_James" title="Ron James">Ron James</a>, and an old guy they randomly picked up who Ice King decides to call <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe" title="Giuseppe">Giuseppe</a> for a road trip. Ron James explains how they'll travel to Big Butt Rock and go on the Cheek's Peak, where the various feelings and insights they gain from the journey will allow them to create a completely original spell, allowing them to form a new school of magic, like a less jerky secret society.
</p><p>The wizards head by the Tree Fort, where Abracadaniel and Ron James (with help from a twinkling-eye potion) get some Water Nymphs to join them as the final members of the journey (Ice King guessed Finn and Jake are out of the wizards' league, though the two rush out to join the trip just as the bus leaves).
</p><p>Their bus runs out of gas in a cow pasture, at which point the wheels also fall off. Ron James figures this is a sign from destiny that they should be inspired by the muddy pasture, so they use the downtime to write unique combinations of words to try to make a new spell. When the gun-toting farmer who owns the land demands they get out, Ice King uses his powers to create an ice road for them to continue onward. Eventually, he runs low on "juice," and trades headgear with Abracadaniel, who continues creating the ice road from the bus' roof.
</p><p>As the bus heads on, Ice King sees a crabapple tree and has Giuseppe get out to get some crabapples. However, the bus can't stop moving, and Giuseppe is too slow to catch up with the bus. While he is able to toss the crabapples to Ice King, he is left behind.
</p><p>In tribute to Giuseppe, Ice King reads Giuseppe's "spell," a heartfelt poem about fathers and daughters, which reduces the wizards to tears. Ice King proclaims it as art. He reads several other "spells" by the wizards, and then calls for a cauldron to combine them into one spell for their arrival to Big Butt Rock. He says an incantation over them, which creates a yellow gas that knocks out everyone, including the returning Abracadaniel, right as the bus enters a swamp.
</p><p>Everyone wakes up later to discover the bus half-sunk in the swamp and filled with water. The <a href="/wiki/Nymph" title="Nymph">nymphs</a> flee, the Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving tries bringing the bus to life (it still can't swim, so he turns it inanimate again) and Ron James tries a vial he got from the <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Mountains" title="Mystery Mountains">Mystery Mountain</a> as a last resort. He drinks some, causing his head to be replaced with <a href="/wiki/Tree_Trunks_(character)" title="Tree Trunks (character)">Tree Trunks</a>, who was being massaged by <a href="/wiki/Mr._Pig" title="Mr. Pig">Mr. Pig</a>, so that he is massaging Ron James' head instead. Ice King tries the potion himself, but only swaps heads with the Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving.
</p><p>Abracadaniel suggests that perhaps, like the rest of the trip, this is destiny guiding them, and they should just accept the situation. With this, Giuseppe appears by the lake, using magic powers to lift the bus out of the lake. Ice King asks Giuseppe how he did this, and Giuseppe winks and gives the wizards a thumbs-up before he turns into blue sparkles that go onto the trees surrounding the lake. Abracadaniel wonders who Giuseppe was, and Ice King replies that he doesn't know, but that Giuseppe showed all the wizards something new. From overhead, it's seen that the sparkles on the trees have formed the shape of Giuseppe's head, which winks.
</p><p>The scene then changes to <a href="/wiki/Wizard_City" title="Wizard City">Wizard City</a>, where a couple of obnoxious wizards exchange a "secret wizard handshake" and openly brag about their "secret thing tonight" in front of Abracadaniel, whom one calls a "loser." As Abracadaniel walks on, he encounters the Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving. They wink at each other and show their rings which have a picture of the crabapple tree on them, a symbol of the society the wizards on the trip have formed based on their unique shared experience. The image on the ring dissolves the crabapple tree that they last saw Giuseppe at and Ice King's voice is heard saying, "Giuseppe."
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>This episode's title is the second longest. (The third longest is "<a href="/wiki/The_Prince_Who_Wanted_Everything" title="The Prince Who Wanted Everything">The Prince Who Wanted Everything</a>," and the first is "<a href="/wiki/Joshua_and_Margaret_Investigations" title="Joshua and Margaret Investigations">Joshua and Margaret Investigations</a>.")
</li><li>The license plate on the bus is "SOM-VY" which is in reference to the writer and storyboard artist of the episode, <a href="/wiki/Somvilay_Xayaphone" title="Somvilay Xayaphone">Somvilay Xayaphone</a>.
</li><li>The Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving's fingers appear much shorter in this episode.
</li><li> Giuseppe's poem was an allusion to him failing as a father by letting his daughter die before him unlike other fathers whose daughters buried their fathers.
</li><li>Abracadaniel put on <a href="/wiki/Ice_King%27s_crown" title="Ice King's crown">Ice King's crown</a> in this episode, but there was no effect on his mentality immediately like with <a href="/wiki/Farmworld_Finn" title="Farmworld Finn">Farmworld Finn</a> in "<a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human_(episode)" title="Finn the Human (episode)">Finn the Human</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog_(episode)" title="Jake the Dog (episode)">Jake the Dog</a>." However, he was able to use the crown's ice magic as soon as he put the crown on. This is because the crown already has a living host, Ice King.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup>
</li><li>This is the first episode where Finn and Jake were willing to hang out with Ice King, although they could have just wanted to hang out with the other wizards or go a road trip.
</li><li>This is the episode where a third water nymph was introduced with the other two from "<a href="/wiki/Power_Animal" title="Power Animal">Power Animal</a>."
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>It is likely the character Giuseppe is named after the Italian artist of the same name, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giuseppe_Arcimboldo" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Giuseppe Arcimboldo">Giuseppe Arcimboldo</a>. This is supported by the fact that the artist mainly worked with fruit and the character Giuseppe is best known for the crabapples he gave to Ice King.
</li><li>Giuseppe appears to have a similar physical appearance to the character <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gollum" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Gollum">Gollum</a> from <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_The_Rings" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:The Lord of The Rings"> The Lord of The Rings</a></i> and <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hobbit" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:The Hobbit">The Hobbit</a></i>.
</li><li>Giuseppe using magic to pull the bus out of the swamp is a reference to Yoda using the force to pull the x-wing out of the swamp in Star Wars Episode V: The Empire Strikes Back.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Little_Dude_(character)" title="Little Dude (character)">Little Dude</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Sleeping_Magi_of_Life_Giving" title="Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving">Ancient Sleeping Magi of Life Giving</a> return from "<a href="/wiki/Little_Dude_(episode)" title="Little Dude (episode)">Little Dude</a>."
<ul><li>Also like in "Little Dude," the Magi used his magic to make something alive (in this case the bus) while saying the word "Mommy."
</li></ul>
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Ron_James" title="Ron James">Ron James</a> returns from "<a href="/wiki/Wizards_Only,_Fools" title="Wizards Only, Fools">Wizards Only, Fools</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Betty_(episode)" title="Betty (episode)">Betty</a>."
</li><li> When the bus is sinking, Ron James takes out his 'last resort Poshe' that he obtained from a mad yogi in the <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Mountains" title="Mystery Mountains">Mystery Mountains</a>, which was a location in the episode '<a href="/wiki/Gotcha!" title="Gotcha!">Gotcha!</a>'
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abracadaniel" title="Abracadaniel">Abracadaniel</a> are still friends from "<a href="/wiki/Play_Date" title="Play Date">Play Date</a>."
</li><li>The <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> finally creating his own secret society culminates the concept of trying (and failing) to get into another society in a story arc built through "<a href="/wiki/Reign_of_Gunters" title="Reign of Gunters">Reign of Gunters</a>," "Wizards Only, Fools," and "Betty."
</li><li>The <a href="/wiki/Old_Ladies" title="Old Ladies">Old Ladies</a> from "<a href="/wiki/The_Enchiridion!_(episode)" title="The Enchiridion! (episode)">The Enchiridion!</a>" return, where they live in a village of suburban houses in the Grass Lands.
</li></ul>
