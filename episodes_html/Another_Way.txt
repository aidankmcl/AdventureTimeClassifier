</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The episode begins with Finn and Jake being treated by Clown Nurses that Jake hired after he and Finn jumped off flying birds and broke their toes. Finn reads in the <a href="/wiki/The_Enchiridion_(book)" title="The Enchiridion (book)">Enchiridion</a> that there is a <a href="/wiki/Forest_Cyclops" title="Forest Cyclops">cyclops</a> whose <a href="/wiki/Cyclops_tears" title="Cyclops tears">tears</a> can heal any injury. He tries to prove that there's another way, but they insist that he leave the healing to them. The nurses wish to cure the duo by kissing their feet, but Finn refuses. The Clown Nurses are determined to 'cure' Finn, so they hold down his arms so he cannot move and kiss his toes. Finn pleads at them to let him go and has a sudden nightmare about the nurses, flips out, yells "MY WAY!!!" and leaves the <a href="/wiki/Tree_Fort" title="Tree Fort">Tree Fort</a>, determined to do things his way. Jake liked the kisses, so he stayed behind.
</p><p>Screaming all the way, Finn runs up to the <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Trees" title="Forest of Trees">Forest of Trees</a> and comes to a fork in the road with a <a href="/wiki/Tree_Stump_With_Sign" title="Tree Stump With Sign">Stump</a> with a direction sign on its head. She tells Finn to choose one of the paths, the left path being <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Trees" title="Forest of Trees">Hair Fall-Out Path</a>, and the path on the right, <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Trees" title="Forest of Trees">Smelly Path</a>. Still haunted by the thought of the Clown Nurses, Finn proclaims he will do things his way, kicks the direction sign off her head, and rushes through the thorn bushes behind her.
</p><p>He comes up to a <a href="/wiki/Acid_River" title="Acid River">river</a>, with a rude <a href="/wiki/Talking_Bush" title="Talking Bush">Talking Bush</a> along the shore. After Finn tells the talking bush of his quest to find the cyclops, the talking bush tells Finn that it is impossible to cross the <a href="/wiki/Acid_River" title="Acid River">river</a>. The river's current is much too fast, the water is acidic, the bridge above the river is a <a href="/wiki/Trap_Bridge" title="Trap Bridge">Trap Bridge</a>, and there are <a href="/wiki/Electric_Eel" title="Electric Eel">electric eels</a> in the water. Still determined to do things his way, Finn rips the Talking Bush out of the ground and runs through the river, swatting the water out of the way with the Talking Bush, but does not even make it to halfway across the river. Ignoring the destroyed Talking Bush's warning to go back, he tosses him to the far bank and runs through the acidic water.
</p><p>Finn rushes up to a little hairy man named <a href="/wiki/Pan" title="Pan">Pan</a> with watermelons in a broken wagon and uses one of the watermelon's juices to rinse off the acidic water. Pan asks him to get his wagon's wheel to out of a tree so he can get the watermelons to his wife down the path, since he was too short to reach them. Finn, having gone mad with doing things his way, smashes the cart and the watermelons roll down the hill and smash the man's house and flatten his wife, <a href="/wiki/Rainy" title="Rainy">Rainy</a>. The distraught Pan tells Finn to go away.
</p><p>Finn, having snapped back to reality, realizes what he has done that day and sings a <a href="/wiki/Melons" title="Melons">sad song</a> in his auto-tune voice on top of a cliff. The cliff, it turns out, is the <a href="/wiki/Forest_Cyclops" title="Forest Cyclops">Forest Cyclops</a>. It tells Finn that it knows he is here for its tears and that Finn will not get any because he never cries, along with stating that he is evil. Finn tells the cyclops he does not want his tears anymore, but the cyclops does not believe him and attacks Finn. Finn tricks the cyclops into bringing its head near him under the pretense of hearing Finn beg for mercy, and punches the cyclops in the eye and causes its tears to fall on him. Finn, now healed, defeats the cyclops and takes its still living head and heals all the people he hurt on his journey (including an inanimate <a href="/wiki/Ham_Sandwich" title="Ham Sandwich">Sandwich</a>, which he brings to life).
</p><p>Back at the <a href="/wiki/Tree_Fort" title="Tree Fort">Tree Fort</a>, Finn tells the Clown Nurses to kiss the cyclops head, which they do. Jake tells Finn he prefers his way of having the nurses kiss his feet, instead of being healed by the tears. Finn accepts that everyone has their own way, much to Jake's amazement.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>This episode reveals that Finn feels uncomfortable around the Clown Nurses.
</li><li>Finn and Jake receive their injuries from jumping off birds. This is depicted in the title card.
</li><li>On the back of the Enchiridion are the numbers "8, 13, 21." These numbers are part of the famed <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fibonacci_sequence" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Fibonacci sequence">Fibonacci sequence</a>, a series of numbers where each number is the sum of the two previous numbers.
<ul><li>Also, in one shot of the book, one paragraph of chapter Ten Cyclops Says: "What Cyclopses lack is depth perception, they make up for it by being really tall and crying a lot. They have pretty bad emotional problems. Cyclops, if you are reading this: Get over it. We all have problems (see appendix B.) but you don't hear us complaining about it. Cyclopses have magic teardrops that possess mystic healing abilities. They are made of a combination of Echinacea, chicken soup, toad potion, apple juice, celery soda, ginseng and marshmallow mold."
</li></ul>
</li><li>The music that plays during the clown nurse's chanting "It's the only way!" is the same as the title card's music.
</li><li> When Finn emerges from the brambles, one of the living trees in the background has an arm, and raises it to its mouth in shock at Finn's appearance.
</li></ul>
