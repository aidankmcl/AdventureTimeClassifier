</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> is in a dream, when he gets sky-boxed and meets his <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">brother</a>. Jake realizes that they must be dreaming at the same time, in similarity to the earlier episode "<a href="/wiki/Crystals_Have_Power" title="Crystals Have Power">Crystals Have Power</a>." Jake suggests that they should meet up in the "awake world". <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> reluctantly agrees and casts a hole dropping Jake through the box, promptly ending his dream. 
</p><p>Jake wakes up and finds <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> working in the kitchen on a strange <a href="/wiki/Harpoon_Launcher" title="Harpoon Launcher">harpoon machine</a>. After the machine malfunctions, ejecting the harpoon. They then agree to go and visit Jermaine. As they leave the treehouse, a scan of the landscape reveals a slug pinned down by the metal harpoon. 
</p><p>The two make it to <a href="/wiki/Joshua_and_Margaret%27s_house" title="Joshua and Margaret's house">Jermaine's house</a> after being chased by <a href="/wiki/Demons" title="Demons">demons</a>, where Jermaine has built a salt barrier that stops the demons from trespassing. Upon arrival, Finn and Jake cause general chaos, much to Jermaine's dismay, and tells them that he has a responsibility to protect all of their <a href="/wiki/Joshua/Quotes" title="Joshua/Quotes">parents</a> strange and valuable loot that he collected, or stole, over the years. Jermaine suddenly looks at his watch and runs away into the hallway.
</p><p>Jermaine heads down a trapdoor, where imprisoned behind a strange blue forcefield is a large blue blob-like monster named <a href="/wiki/Bryce" title="Bryce">Bryce</a>. Jermaine flips a cassette tape in a bear, <a href="/wiki/Booboo_Sousa" title="Booboo Sousa">Booboo Sousa</a>, hooked up to wires in the wall that seems to project the forcefield, and from the conversation it is implied that the ongoing song is what keeps Bryce imprisoned. Bryce threatens to eat Jermaine "from the bottom up", and it is revealed that Bryce is being held prisoner after trying to steal back his poster that Joshua originally stole from Bryce many years ago. Jermaine remarks that he would have let Bryce go with his poster years ago had it not been for all of the psychotic things Bryce says. Bryce threatens Jermaine again, and the brothers go upstairs and make fried rice. 
</p><p>While eating the rice, Jermaine asks Jake what he put in the fried rice. Jake reveals he used sesame oil and salt that he got from the salt barrier. This leads to one of the demons crossing the barrier, and the three quickly make plans to destroy it. However their plans go awry, and Jermaine ends up having to suck the demon up with a vacuum. After sucking the demon up the vacuum, Jermaine runs outside where he chucks the vacuum in one of the other demon's face and fixes the salt barrier. 
</p><p>After the fight, Jermaine begins to get angry and reveals his jealous toward Finn and Jake's do-whatever lifestyle and how he has to stay behind and watch his dad's old treasures. Jermaine then proceeds to assault Jake. As Jermaine tosses random objects at Jake, one combusts and sets the house on fire. Jermaine continues to punch Jake, who lets out a fart which causes Jermaine to laugh and see that "he's built his own cage." Finn tries to get the three of them to work together to put the fire out, but Jermaine, who's come to terms with his life, decides it is okay to let the house burn down
</p><p>.  
</p><p>Once the house burns, the scene changes to daytime and the demons are gone. Jake suggests they are probably gone because their stuff was burned in the fire, giving them no reason to stay to get it back. Jermaine's watch alarm goes off, and he runs over to the trapdoor to find Bryce crawling out clutching his poster. Jermaine and Bryce seem to be completely normal, and walk off together into the woods, with Jermaine nagging at Bryce for being cynical and pessimistic about life. Finn and Jake stare at them from the house ruins as the episode ends. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li> This is the first episode to feature <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> outside of holograms, flashbacks, or memories.
</li><li> <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> gets a slight redesign in this episode. He has no lips and his teeth are perfectly aligned.
</li><li> This is the first time <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> is seen with normal eyeballs (within the dreamworld).
</li><li> After <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn's</a> <a href="/wiki/Harpoon_Launcher" title="Harpoon Launcher">machine</a> exploded, the harpoon attached to it had flown off and crashed through the roof. We later see Finn and Jake travel to Jermaine's house and a giant slug having the giant harpoon impaled in its back.
</li><li>This episode reveals that <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> stills lives in <a href="/wiki/Joshua_and_Margaret%27s_house" title="Joshua and Margaret's house">his parents' house</a> even after their death.
<ul><li>It also revealed that the house from "<a href="/wiki/Joshua_and_Margaret_Investigations" title="Joshua and Margaret Investigations">Joshua and Margaret Investigations</a>" is not the one seen in "<a href="/wiki/Memory_of_a_Memory" title="Memory of a Memory">Memory of a Memory</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Pit" title="The Pit">The Pit</a>." They most likely moved into a bigger house because their children were born.
</li></ul>
</li><li> A box labeled "T.V." can be seen on the shelf of magical items Jermaine throws at Jake.
</li><li> In Jake's Dream, the ears of Jermaine's bear suit have images of tombstones with the letters "J" and "M." This is likely referring to Joshua and Margaret, his two deceased parents.
</li><li> The Booboo Sousa song plays in the end credits of this episode and "<a href="/wiki/Chips_and_Ice_Cream" title="Chips and Ice Cream">Chips and Ice Cream</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[4]</a></sup>
<ul><li>This is the second time a different song replaces the original <i><a href="/wiki/Island_Song" title="Island Song" class="mw-redirect">Island song</a></i>, the first being in the end credits of the episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Suitor" title="The Suitor">The Suitor</a>." (<a href="/wiki/Braco_Don%27t_Go" title="Braco Don't Go">Braco Don't Go</a>)
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li> One of the demons that tried to attack Finn and Jake opened his mouth, revealing a second mouth inside. This is similar to the Xenomorph from the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alien_(franchise)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Alien (franchise)">Alien</a></i> series.
</li><li> The giant sword that <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> is seen carrying, and then smashing a table with, is a reference to Cloud Strife's Buster Sword from <i>Final Fantasy VII</i>.
</li><li> When <a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> tells <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> to leave the house, he refers to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groucho_Marx" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Groucho Marx">Groucho Marx</a>, the well-known 20th century American vaudevillian.
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> takes what looks like a vacuum to suck up the <a href="/wiki/Demons" title="Demons">demons</a>, which is an allusion to the <i>Ghostbusters</i> franchise.
<ul><li>This may also be an allusion to the video game <i>Luigi's Mansion.</i>
</li></ul>
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Jermaine" title="Jermaine">Jermaine</a> used <a href="/wiki/Booboo_Sousa" title="Booboo Sousa">Booboo Sousa</a> to keep <a href="/wiki/Bryce" title="Bryce">Bryce</a> at bay, which is a homage to Teddy Ruxpin.
</li><li>Using salt to repel spirits and demons is most likely an allusion to the <i>Supernatural</i> series, though it is an old legend.
</li><li>Comedy duo <a rel="nofollow" class="external text exitstitial" href="http://stereolaffs.com/">Scharpling &amp; Wurster</a> appear together as the voices of Jermaine and Bryce respectively. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Scharpling" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Tom Scharpling">Tom Scharpling</a>, the voice of Jermaine, is more well known as the voice of Greg Universe on <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Universe" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Steven Universe">Steven Universe</a></i>.
</li><li>The rug on the floor of the living room has an image of the Tree of Life from the Kabbalah.
</li><li>The rose under a glass covering that can be seen as Jermaine goes to flip the cassette looks like the enchanted rose from "Beauty and the Beast".
</li></ul>
