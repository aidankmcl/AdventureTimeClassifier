<p>The show starts off with Finn giving drinks to everybody at their <a href="/wiki/Finn_and_Jake_Movie_Club" title="Finn and Jake Movie Club">movie club</a>, a weekly gathering where Finn and Jake show old movies they found that are from before the <a href="/wiki/Mushroom_War" title="Mushroom War">Mushroom War</a>. Everyone then gets ready for the movie, with <a href="/wiki/Lumpy_Space_Princess" title="Lumpy Space Princess">Lumpy Space Princess</a> complaining that <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a> took her seat. Once the movie starts, Princess Bubblegum notices the FBI warning for arrest of copyrighting. Finn and Jake then cancel movie night due to the fact that they believe they have been illegally showing copyrighted movies.     
<a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> says not to worry, because the movies were made before the <a href="/wiki/Mushroom_War" title="Mushroom War">Mushroom War</a>, and therefore the copyright technically has no power, but <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> decides to respect authority and not show them anymore.
</p><p>After everyone leaves, Finn and Jake find an <a href="/wiki/Video_camera" title="Video camera">old video camera</a> and decide to start filming their own movie. The next day they start filming the inhabitants of Ooo, but realize that this is completely random and will never make a real movie, so they must come up with a plot. While Jake wants it to be a romantic comedy, Finn wants an action adventure. This turns out be a problem, because every piece of footage that Jake likes, Finn hates and vice versa. All of the movie club is the cast, and once they are finally done chasing bullfrogs and filming weddings, BMO is left to do the editing. While waiting they again start to argue about whether the movie should be a romance or an action, since about half of the footage is the former and the other half the latter. They ask BMO which clips are best but he changes his screen to "back in 5 minutes" to avoid answering. On movie night, with no idea how the movie will turn out, Finn and Jake present it to movie club in a very disgruntled attitude toward each other. Instead of the footage, however, it turns out that BMO cut the <i>entire</i> movie and has replaced it with his own animation which depicts all of the good times that Finn and Jake shared during the film's production, and he sings a song about how Finn and Jake should get along. Everyone loves the movie and Finn and Jake see how silly they were in fighting and make up. <a href="/wiki/BMO" title="BMO">BMO</a> says Jake's favorite recurring line throughout the original filming, "Check, please," and everyone laughs.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>All of the <a href="/wiki/Category:Main_Characters" title="Category:Main Characters">main characters</a> appear in this episode except <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a>.
</li><li>While looking for the video recorder, Finn pulls out a holographic projector which projects a spherical <a href="/wiki/PHIL_FACE" title="PHIL FACE">PHIL FACE</a>.
</li><li>This is the first episode in the series to mention the <a href="/wiki/Mushroom_War" title="Mushroom War">Mushroom War</a> by name.
</li><li>When Finn was pouring drinks he poured several different drinks with the same pitcher.
<ul><li>The pitcher has an inscription of Futhorc (Anglo-Saxon) runes on it. According to the <a href="/wiki/Runic_code" title="Runic code">Runic code</a> it says, "Cup of Many Ale[s]."
</li></ul>
</li><li>When <i>Heat Signature 3</i> begins playing, "Document 413" is displayed briefly followed by "Classified." Despite Finn and Jake reading the standard FBI warning on copyright, what is written is not an FBI warning but rather what appears to be a warning on disclosing classified information. Also, it misspells "strictly" as "stricktly".
</li><li>When Finn and Jake's movie starts to play, it shows that the production studio was "BFF Productions" with Finn and Jake's faces as the logo.
</li><li>This is the final episode before the <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snail</a> becomes possessed by the <a href="/wiki/Lich" title="Lich" class="mw-redirect">Lich</a>.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>The phrase "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text exitstitial" href="http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/CheckPlease">Check Please</a>" is a punchline used in many TV shows and movies, usually after something off-beat happens.
</li><li>The way Finn made the tomato juice for Marceline is similar to the style of a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloody_Mary_(cocktail)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Bloody Mary (cocktail)">Bloody Mary</a> cocktail, because there was a celery stalk in the drink.
</li><li>Finn was using an optical illusion, known as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/forced_perspective" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:forced perspective">forced perspective</a>, when he was shooting the chariot scene with Princess Bubblegum.
</li><li>The document number for <i>Heat Signature 3</i> could be a reference to the webcomic <i>Homestuck</i>, where the number 413 appears multiple times.
</li><li>During the wedding scene, Shelby wears a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/clerical_collar" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:clerical collar">clerical collar</a>, something often worn by Christian priests.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li>During the second movie club meeting, Princess Bubblegum is seen wearing the first outfit she wore in "<a href="/wiki/The_Real_You" title="The Real You">The Real You</a>," and Marceline is seen wearing her outfit from "<a href="/wiki/It_Came_from_the_Nightosphere" title="It Came from the Nightosphere">It Came from the Nightosphere</a>."
</li><li>The name of the film at the beginning of the episode was <i>Heat Signature 3</i>, the third film in a series. The original <i>Heat Signature</i> is a plot device in <a href="/wiki/Heat_Signature_(episode)" title="Heat Signature (episode)">the episode of the same name</a>.
</li><li>The soundtrack from the part where Jake is luring the frog using a bait with a fly is also been used in the opening titlecard of "<a href="/wiki/Gut_Grinder_(episode)" title="Gut Grinder (episode)">Gut Grinder</a>."
</li><li>Shelby's punchline and his apparent priestly profession reappears in the fifth season episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Pit" title="The Pit">The Pit</a>".
</li></ul>
