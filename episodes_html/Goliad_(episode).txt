<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span></h2>
<p>Princess Bubblegum is concerned over her mortality, and reveals her designated successor to Finn and Jake. However, things get out of control when the heir turns against Princess Bubblegum.

</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>While <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> are doing activities near the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kingdom" title="Candy Kingdom">Candy Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peppermint_Butler" title="Peppermint Butler">Peppermint Butler</a> informs them that <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a> wants to see them. The princess explains that after her <a href="/wiki/Mortal_Folly" title="Mortal Folly">brush with death at the hands of the Lich</a>, she realized she cannot live forever, so she used science and some of her DNA to create an heir that could live forever. The heir is <a href="/wiki/Goliad_(character)" title="Goliad (character)">Goliad</a>, a pink <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sphinx" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Sphinx">Sphinx</a> with a mound on her forehead and the voice of a young British child. Finn and Jake introduce themselves to Goliad, who takes a liking to them. After Princess Bubblegum plucks one of Finn's hairs, he notices that she is extremely exhausted. She explains that she has been awake for 83 hours straight, teaching Goliad about being a good ruler. Finn and Jake volunteer to look after Goliad while the princess rests, and she agrees.
</p><p>The two friends take the curious sphinx to a preschool, where Finn builds an <a href="/wiki/Obstacle_course" title="Obstacle course">obstacle course</a>. Jake introduces her to the other children, but is dismayed to find that they are extremely unruly and poorly behaved. Jake is forced to yell at the children to make them stop assaulting him. Later Goliad imitates Jake's harsh discipline, yelling at the children in an attempt at being a leader. Finn rebukes Goliad, and suggests that she use her brain to lead instead of force. Goliad reveals that her mound concealed a third eye, and proceeds to psychically control Finn and the obstacles in the course for a perfect completion. She explains that with her in control, everything would be perfect. Worried about Goliad's behavior, Finn and Jake take her to the princess for help.
</p><p>Princess Bubblegum tries to teach Goliad that compromise is the best way to rule, citing how a bee and a flower help each other. Goliad counters by explaining that the bee does not care if it hurts or kills the flower because the flower is weaker. Goliad states that she is the strongest so she is in control. Princess Bubblegum thinks to herself that her creation has been corrupted and would need to be disassembled and started from scratch, but Goliad reveals she can read minds and begins to destroy the castle. Princess Bubblegum tells Finn and Jake that Goliad is too powerful to be defeated by them. She instructs them to distract Goliad long enough to create another Candy Sphinx to fight back, and that they need to not think about the plan or Goliad will find out.
</p><p>Finn and Jake confront Goliad but are beaten by her psychic powers. Goliad tries to read Finn's mind, and Finn narrowly avoids revealing the plan by interrupting his memories with nonsense. The new creature; another sphinx with an eagle's head, white feathers, and golden hair; rescues Finn and battles Goliad. Goliad tries to convince her brother, named <a href="/wiki/Stormo" title="Stormo">Stormo</a>, that they should work together, but Stormo refuses by screeching. They engage in a psychic showdown, but with their powers matched the two creatures are eternally locked in a mental stalemate. Princess Bubblegum tells Finn that Stormo was created using DNA from the hair she took earlier, and thus inherited Finn's heroic qualities. That was why he saved Finn instead of controlling them, and why he sacrificed himself to keep Goliad in check for all eternity. Finn realizes that Stormo is like a son to him, and wishes him a Happy Birthday.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>The ingredients to create Goliad include: various candies, amino acids, "algebra," and Princess Bubblegum's baby tooth (DNA). Cinnamon Bun also fell into the vat, but escaped immediately after.
<ul><li>The algebra Princess Bubblegum uses to create Goliad is "A+B=C." What actually gets added to the vat is simple chalk dust.
</li></ul>
</li><li>When Princess Bubblegum, Finn, and Jake are walking to the underground lab, fossils of strange animals can be seen.
</li><li>On Princess Bubblegum's chalkboard, she has drawn something similar to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/carbon_trioxide" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:carbon trioxide">carbon trioxide</a>, but it is in an impossible oxidation state.
</li><li>Princess Bubblegum did not wear her <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum%27s_crown" title="Princess Bubblegum's crown">royal crown</a> for the entire episode. However, it was seen on her in Finn's mind when Goliad was trying to read it.
</li><li>When Finn and Jake enter the preschool, one of the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kid" title="Candy Kid">Candy Kids</a> climbed through a window up into the roof.
</li><li>This is the first time Princess Bubblegum wears glasses.
</li><li>When Finn and Jake enter the preschool, one of the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kid" title="Candy Kid">Candy Kids</a> harassing the caretaker is waving a toy truck with おいしい [oishii], which is the Japanese word for "delicious," written on it. Later Jake sends him flying through a window up in the roof when he explodes in anger after they tried to get his brain and eyes.
</li><li>In the fight between Finn's mind and Goliad, Princess Bubblegum says, "She's far too powerful," although she did not say that in talking about the plan when Goliad stormed into the castle.
</li><li>In the preschool, when the small marshmallow child is stuffing his mouth with blocks, a doll that looks like Princess Bubblegum can be seen in the cubbyhole on the left of him.
</li><li>The candy people Jake spit up at the end are <a href="/wiki/Chet" title="Chet">Chet</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Marshmallow_Kids" title="Marshmallow Kids">marshmallow kid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peppermint_Butler" title="Peppermint Butler">Peppermint Butler</a>. They are not seen anywhere near the candy tower after Jake spits them up; this could be an error or they just fell down which could have probably killed them or leave them seriously injured.
</li><li>Goliad's fur is the same color as Princess Bubblegum's hair and skin color.
</li><li>In the lab where Goliad is introduced, there is a poster on the wall with a similar layout to the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/periodic_table" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:periodic table">periodic table</a>, but it is labeled "Fructology."
</li><li>In a scene at the beginning of the episode when Princess Bubblegum is walking down the stairs it can be seen that she is not wearing shoes.
</li><li>Among the candy kids there is one that bears resemblance to Peppermint Butler, although his eyes are black instead of white as Peppermint's are and his arms and legs are white unlike Peppermint's that are red.
</li><li>This is the first time Jake exploded on rage.
</li><li>Jake always has curved and missing teeth when yelling.
</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>"Goliad" is the name of a town in Texas where the second battle of the Texas Revolution took place. <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Herpich" title="Thomas Herpich">Tom Herpich</a> thought the town had a cool-sounding name, and he decided to name the character after it.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>
</li><li>Both Goliad and Stormo resemble <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/sphinx" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:sphinx">sphinxes</a>, creatures that in legend possess attributes of three creatures: the human, the lion and a large bird. While Goliad and Stormo both have lion-like bodies, the bird and humanoid features vary between them.
</li><li>The stalemate between Goliad and Stormo resembles the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mythology_of_Lost" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Mythology of Lost">mythology of <i>Lost</i></a>. The concept of two deities in an eternal stalemate is also found in various religions. This is one of <i>Adventure Time'</i>s many hints towards mysticism.
</li></ul>
