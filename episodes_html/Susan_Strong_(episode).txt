<p><a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> is excited to discover a tribe of "humans" living underground, but gets more than he expected when he tries to teach one of them about the surface world.

</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>Finn and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> are asked to do a favor for <a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a> by ripping out taffy-tree stumps. Before they start taking the stumps out, Princess Bubblegum inadvertently brings up some complex emotions in Finn by reminding him that he is the only known human. Though this fact does not outwardly bother Finn, he claims that when he thinks about it too much, he gets "all soul-searchy and weird." To take his mind off this thought, Finn and Jake start work uprooting. Very soon after they begin to work they find a strange metallic hatch among the old tree stumps. They hop down to find an underground cave full of garbage. Living in the garbage are a tribe of "<a href="/wiki/Hyooman_tribe" title="Hyooman tribe">Hyoomans</a>" who wear animal-themed hats on their heads (similar to Finn's) and are scared of their own shadows and practically everything else, so Finn explains that the things they were afraid of would not hurt them.
</p><p>Finn corners an unusually strong woman with a cat hat. He is stunned and excited to see what he thinks is another human. He attempts to encourage her to come to the surface and see the sun. When asked her name, she says "su-sun" trying to repeat what Finn had said earlier about the sun. Finn misinterprets her statement as Susan, or perhaps it is her actual name coincidentally. He brings her to the surface world to teach her about the wonders it holds. This is tough at first, as Susan is afraid of everything she encounters. After several occasions of Susan being afraid of harmless things such as rocks (which Jake refers to as "low-level enemies") and a <a href="/wiki/Pincer_Beetle" title="Pincer Beetle">Dancing Bug</a>, Finn and Jake roast marshmallows with her.
</p><p>As Jake plays guitar, Finn sings a <a href="/wiki/Susan_Strong_(song)" title="Susan Strong (song)">song</a> to Susan about life on the surface. Seeing that Susan really likes the marshmallows, Finn takes her to the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kingdom" title="Candy Kingdom">Candy Kingdom</a>. There she talks to Princess Bubblegum and has apparently learned basic English. Overwhelmed by the abundance of sweet, delicious candy, she attempts to eat <a href="/wiki/Peppermint_Butler" title="Peppermint Butler">Peppermint Butler</a> until she is stopped by Finn who says "you can't eat the ones that talk!" After Finn scolds her for trying to eat the Candy People, she realizes that Peppermint Butler is a friend and believes that everything, besides Peppermint Butler, is edible and acceptable to eat.
</p><p>She runs to inform the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Hyooman_tribe" title="Hyooman tribe">Hyooman</a> tribe to eat the entire Candy Kingdom except for Peppermint Butler. Finn hurriedly warns the Princess about Susan and the others. She tries to make the <a href="/wiki/Candy_People" title="Candy People">candy people</a> scary but there is no masking their cuteness. Remembering that shadows look scary to the Hyoomans, Finn and Jake show them the spooky shadows of the Candy People. This works at first, but Susan, who knows better and has learned enough that she can now teach her tribemates, explains to them that it is safe, and they continue the attack. However, the young <a href="/wiki/Marshmallow_Kids" title="Marshmallow Kids">Marshmallow Kids</a>, who feel like they can take on the Hyoomans, are accidentally set on fire and run after the Hyoomans, setting them on fire in the process.
</p><p>They throw off their burning hats to reveal that they are actually mutated humans with gills and fins. Finn is in a state of shock when he realizes that they are not true humans. Finn asks Susan, who was smart enough to avoid the fire, if she is really a human. Instead of answering she runs away looking confused and scared. Finn wonders aloud if she was a human or a wild animal, and Jake tells him "we're all wild animals, brother." Finn then eats some of the burned marshmallow skin and replies, "Yeah... I guess we are, brother."
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>This is the only episode to have its storyboard released before the episode aired.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>
</li><li>In this episode it is revealed that not only the inhabitants, but all the buildings and even the ground in the <a href="/wiki/Candy_Kingdom" title="Candy Kingdom">Candy Kingdom</a> are edible.
</li><li>When Finn and Jake alert Princess Bubblegum that Susan and the Hyoomans will try to eat her people, a painting of Princess Bubblegum and her grandmother can be seen on the wall of the room.
</li><li>It is revealed in this episode that Jake likes his marshmallows toasted golden-brown and Finn likes his burnt black.
</li><li>Besides playing a viola, Jake also plays a guitar.
</li><li>The Nuclear Bunkers the Hyoomans live in are similar to the Fallout series' Vaults, which are also used to protect the inhabitants from Nuclear weapons. However, most of them failed, which may suggest the "Fish People" being created in a similar way - from exposure to radiation due to the Great Mushroom War.
</li><li>This is the last episode before Finn turns 13 years old.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li>There is a <a href="/wiki/Scientific_Parasite" title="Scientific Parasite">Scientific Parasite</a> in Princess Bubblegum's purse, which was seen first in "<a href="/wiki/His_Hero" title="His Hero">His Hero</a>."
</li><li>When Finn and Jake take Susan to the Red Rock Pass, it appears that the rock Finn headbutted off a cliff in an attempt to hit Marceline's dad below (during a scene from "<a href="/wiki/It_Came_from_the_Nightosphere" title="It Came from the Nightosphere">It Came from the Nightosphere</a>") was somehow returned to its initial place. Susan destroys this same rock by squeezing it with her powerful arms and legs.
</li><li>When Finn and Jake take Susan to the surface, she gets scared of the grass, and Finn tells her, "Grass can't hurt you." However, this is ironic, because the <a href="/wiki/Grass_Sword" title="Grass Sword">Grass Sword</a> he bought in "<a href="/wiki/Blade_of_Grass" title="Blade of Grass">Blade of Grass</a>" is ultimately what caused him to lose his right arm in "<a href="/wiki/Escape_from_the_Citadel" title="Escape from the Citadel">Escape from the Citadel</a>."
</li></ul>
