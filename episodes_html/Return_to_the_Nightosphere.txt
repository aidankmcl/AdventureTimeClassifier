<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hunson_Abadeer" title="Hunson Abadeer">Marceline's dad</a> locks up <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> in a prison in the <a href="/wiki/Nightosphere_(location)" title="Nightosphere (location)">Nightosphere</a>, and they can't remember why. Now, they must find a way to escape the Nightosphere. Part 1 of a two-part episode.

</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The episode begins with Finn and Jake waking up in a pile of <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">bananas</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Nightosphere_Jail" title="Nightosphere Jail">jail cell</a> with their memories of how they arrived there completely erased. The <a href="/wiki/Demon_Guard" title="Demon Guard">jail guard</a> comes and tells the two that they're in the Nightosphere. After asking how they can get home, he explains that they cannot unless <a href="/wiki/Hunson_Abadeer" title="Hunson Abadeer">Hunson Abadeer</a>  allows them. Finn and Jake, along with other <a href="/wiki/Demons" title="Demons">demons</a>, are then set free because they cannot leave the Nightosphere anyway. They climb up through a hole and look around to get a good view of the Nightosphere. Afterwards, Finn and Jake ask for help from a <a href="/wiki/Half_Monster_Demon" title="Half Monster Demon">Half Monster Demon</a>, who tells them to climb into him and he will carry them to <a href="/wiki/Teller" title="Teller" class="mw-redirect">the Teller</a>.
</p><p>After being dropped off at a river full of demons, a <a href="/wiki/Demons#Boat_Demon" title="Demons">traveler</a> explains to them that they have to wait in a line. They spend almost 28 days waiting for their turn to see the Teller (though the time elapse could have just been a joke). When it is finally their turn, they have to stand in another long line. In that moment, a storm cloud appears in a mist and starts killing all the demons that were there to ask Hunson Abadeer questions, so Jake and Finn hide behind a rock. They barely escape, and follow the cloud to its castle. There, they fight their way back to the portal in order to get home.
</p><p>They go into the portal and Hunson Abadeer follows them. Finn fights him and cuts his head with his <a href="/wiki/Demon_Sword" title="Demon Sword" class="mw-redirect">Demon Sword</a>, which reveals that the ruler was really <a href="/wiki/Marceline" title="Marceline">Marceline</a> all along. When they get back to <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Ooo" title="Land of Ooo">Ooo</a>, Marceline closes the <a href="/wiki/Nightosphere_portal" title="Nightosphere portal">Nightosphere portal</a> and tells them to never come back. Finn claims they must go back to discover the truth.
</p><p>This episode continues with its second part: "<a href="/wiki/Daddy%27s_Little_Monster" title="Daddy's Little Monster">Daddy's Little Monster</a>."
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>It is revealed in this episode that Marceline's dad has an actual name, Hunson Abadeer. Throughout the episode, Finn and Jake refer to him as "Hunson."
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> can be seen with the <a href="/wiki/Hunson_Abadeer" title="Hunson Abadeer">Lord of Evil</a> in a picture, this being the first time Abe is shown in the actual series.
</li><li>Another picture shows <a href="/wiki/Marceline" title="Marceline">Marceline</a> as a child standing next to her father on the left and another woman standing in the right who has a striking resemblance to Marceline. This may be her mother.
</li><li>In one of the pictures, <a href="/wiki/Peppermint_Butler" title="Peppermint Butler">Peppermint Butler</a> is seen playing golf with the Lord of Evil.
</li><li>Jake spoke in Japanese in this episode - albeit broken. After cheering up Finn, he said "‘(上手で頑張ってね）Jouzu de Ganbatte ne" - which roughly translates as "Have faith and go forth!"<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>
</li><li>Some demons mispronounce the Lord of Evil's last name, saying "Aberdeer."
</li><li><a href="/wiki/John_DiMaggio" title="John DiMaggio">John DiMaggio</a> (the voice of Jake) performs the voice of the Teller in this episode.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span></h3>
<ul><li>When Marceline reveals herself Finn's sword disappears, it reappears when she tells them to stay away from her forever.
</li><li> As Jake says "Hunson Abadeer? Heheh.. Alright.", his mouth is not synced with his dialogue. He appears to be saying "Hunson Abadeer? Heheh.. Funny."
</li><li> When the Boat Demon is first shown, his eyes are black. However, in all of his subsequent scenes, his eyes are green.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>The appearance of The Nightosphere and its denizens appear to be directly inspired by the paintings of 16th century artist <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hieronymus_Bosch" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Hieronymus Bosch">Hieronymus Bosch</a>. Particularly the third panel of his triptych painting <i>The Garden of Earthly Delights</i>.
</li><li>During the first recording of the Nightosphere, a creature floats by that looks similar to a Ghast from the sandbox game <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minecraft" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Minecraft">Minecraft</a>.
</li><li>As the "blood mist cloud" retracts into the tower after attacking the line, a clinking sound can be heard, reminiscent of the sound heard as the smoke monster on <i>Lost</i> retreats.
</li><li>The boat demon rowing over the other demons' heads is similar to the demon Phleygas, in Dante Alighieri's poem <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inferno_(Dante)#Fifth_Circle_.28Anger.29" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Inferno (Dante)">Inferno</a></i>, who ferries over the river Styx, which is filled with wrathful and sullen sinners.
</li><li>The whole beginning sequence of them forgetting how they got somewhere is a reference to the movie <i>Hangover</i>. Later on they find some videos on Jake's flipphone, which is also a reference to the ending of <i>Hangover</i>.
</li><li>During the panorama scene, a line of cloaked and hooded figures can be seen climbing up a tower and then leaping off, while chanting is heard. This bears a resemblance to the animation in Monty Python and the Holy Grail when a line of monks each jumps in succession from a diving board while chanting.
</li><li>Finn and Jake have 42.000.000 number in the queue. Meanwhile, "42" is an "Answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything" from <i>The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy</i> created by Adams Douglas.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li>The hair style that <a href="/wiki/Marceline" title="Marceline">Marceline</a> wears is the same as in "<a href="/wiki/It_Came_from_the_Nightosphere" title="It Came from the Nightosphere">It Came From the Nightosphere</a>," which is coincidental because <a href="/wiki/Hunson_Abadeer" title="Hunson Abadeer">Hunson Abadeer</a> was in both episodes.
</li><li>After Finn and Jake have been released from their cage in the dungeon, a creature that resembles a <a href="/wiki/Lemon_People" title="Lemon People">Lemon Child</a> can be seen writhing in the background.
</li><li>The presentation of the Nightosphere was similar to the <a href="/wiki/Land_of_the_Dead" title="Land of the Dead">Land of the Dead</a> in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Death_in_Bloom" title="Death in Bloom">Death in Bloom</a>." Also, the song played when F&amp;J entered the <a href="/wiki/Half_Monster_Demon" title="Half Monster Demon">Half Monster Demon</a>, were the same played during the same scene of the presentation of the Land of the Dead.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_notes">Production notes</span></h3>
<ul><li>This episode was previously titled "They Went to the Nightosphere," and was renamed.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup> Some sites such as T.V. Guide and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dish_Network" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dish Network">Dish Network</a> still used the old title. The current title was used as a former title for the episode "<a href="/wiki/It_Came_from_the_Nightosphere" title="It Came from the Nightosphere">It Came from the Nightosphere</a>."
</li><li>This is part one of a two-part episode, and originally aired together with "<a href="/wiki/Daddy%27s_Little_Monster" title="Daddy's Little Monster">Daddy's Little Monster</a>."
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Storyline_analysis">Storyline analysis</span></h3>
<ul><li>There are subtle indications that the Nightosphere ruler is a different entity from Hunson Abadeer, which may help viewers to guess that its identity is Marceline. Compared to her father in their respective Nightosphere forms, Marceline's demonic head is noticeably smaller, has more tentacles, and some features of her head and suit have different colors.
</li><li>Hunson Abadeer's picture with Peppermint Butler further proves Peppermint's connection with 'evil'. In "<a href="/wiki/Death_in_Bloom" title="Death in Bloom">Death in Bloom</a>" it is revealed that he knows <a href="/wiki/Death" title="Death">Death</a>, besides that at the end of the episode he told Finn and Jake he wanted their flesh as payment for helping them and that he would take it from them while they sleep as his eyes started to turn somewhat demonic (although he never actually did that to them). Also, in "<a href="/wiki/You_Made_Me" title="You Made Me">You Made Me</a>" when Finn wants to see Peppermint's aura he says "You don't wanna see that!" implying that they will not like what they will see and/or that he does not want them to know his true nature.
</li></ul>
