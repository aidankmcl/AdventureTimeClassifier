<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jake" title="Jake">Jake</a> are excited to meet the <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Nice King</a> who has come to <a href="/wiki/Land_of_Ooo" title="Land of Ooo">Ooo</a>, but when he starts giving orders, they realize he is the <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a>, only with a shaven beard.

</script><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span></h2>
<p>The story opens with Finn and Jake saving <a href="/wiki/Engagement_Ring_Princess" title="Engagement Ring Princess">Engagement Ring Princess</a> from the sleeping Ice King. Upon waking up, the Ice King is infuriated, convinced "She broke up with (him)" before becoming depressed on what went wrong. He eventually shaves all of his beard off and attempts to work out in a <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a>. He is approached by <a href="/wiki/Slime_Princess" title="Slime Princess">Slime Princess</a>, who falls in love with him, mistaking him for a "<a href="/wiki/Ice_King#Nice_King" title="Ice King">Nice King</a>" which was actually a misunderstanding in which Ice King says; "Well I'm an Ice King." Ice King attempted to correct the mistake, but when he realizes that Slime Princess likes him as the Nice King, he goes along with the misunderstanding, but instead, he mentioned he was a "really Nice King." <a href="/wiki/Lumpy_Space_Princess" title="Lumpy Space Princess">Lumpy Space Princess</a> spies on them and calls all the other <a href="/wiki/Princess_(disambiguation)" title="Princess (disambiguation)">princesses</a>.
</p><p>Meanwhile, Finn and Jake are walking home from killing a cyclops when <a href="/wiki/Princess_Princess_Princess" title="Princess Princess Princess">Princess Princess Princess</a> bumps into them and mentions the Nice King. Finn and Jake decide to be the Nice King's knights.
</p><p>Back at the sand pit, Nice King has built himself a sand castle using the elements of the playground and collects all of the admiring princesses in a makeshift cage as Finn and Jake arrive. He knights them and orders them to sort through his admirers to find a suitably good looking princess he can marry. During this process, Finn begins to feel guilty for Jake's turning down Ghost Princess and Turtle Princess and making them cry. He tries to explain his feelings to the Nice King, who notices his own beard is growing back. Finn and Jake are instructed to go to the Ice Kingdom to retrieve his "stolen" razor.
</p><p>Finn and Jake find a razor, but also discover the Ice King's shaven beard. They eventually realize the Nice King is the Ice King and rush back. The princesses stand in their way, refusing to let them beat up the Nice King. The heroes manage to push past them by using Jake's ear as a shield. After a chase scene through the sand castle, Finn kicks Nice King out of a tower and the two fall. Jake jumps down after them and Finn glues the beard back on the Ice King, revealing his true identity to the princesses. Angry, they all beat the Ice King up.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span></h2>
<ul><li>The picture that Ice King breaks with his banana-phone is of him and Gunter posing in front of Engagement Ring Princess while she is in his ice prison and the frame says "Together Forever."
</li><li>The sound heard while Finn and Jake were climbing the sand castle after the Nice King is the same music in the title card.
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Natasha_Allegri" title="Natasha Allegri">Natasha Allegri</a> did a princess <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/walla" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:walla">walla</a> in this episode.
</li><li>The "Nice King's" castle is built out of sand from the playground's sandbox.
</li><li>The question, "Do you like drums?" is a reference to the <a href="/wiki/Ice_King%27s_instruments" title="Ice King's instruments">drum set</a> always seen in the Ice King's lair.
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Princess_Bubblegum" title="Princess Bubblegum">Princess Bubblegum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flame_Princess" title="Flame Princess">Flame Princess</a> were the only main princesses who did not appear in this episode, though Flame Princess had not yet been introduced.
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Jungle_Princess" title="Jungle Princess">Jungle Princess</a> was shown the second time in the title card.
</li><li>It is revealed in this episode that <a href="/wiki/Engagement_Ring_Princess" title="Engagement Ring Princess">Engagement Ring Princess</a> likes the opera.
</li><li><a href="/wiki/Lumpy_Space_Princess" title="Lumpy Space Princess">Lumpy Space Princess</a> lied to <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> because <a href="/wiki/Ice_King" title="Ice King">Ice King</a> said he doesn't like her.
</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h3>
<ul><li>The Ice King's "Nice King" appearance resembles <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mister_Rogers%27_Neighborhood" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Mister Rogers' Neighborhood">Mr. Rogers</a>'.
</li><li>The part where Jake goes up and down is similar to the way <a href="http://mario.wikia.com/wiki/Yoshi" class="extiw" title="w:c:mario:Yoshi">Yoshi</a> does in the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Mario_(series)" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Super Mario (series)">Super Mario</a> series.
</li><li>Ice King's banana phone may be a reference to the Raffi song <i>Bananaphone.</i>
</li></ul>
