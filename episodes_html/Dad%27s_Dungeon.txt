<p>Finn, <a href="/wiki/BMO" title="BMO">BMO</a> and Jake are at the boat of the tree fort, telling Jake what they want him to turn into. Finn suggests a cheetah farting, BMO suggests a cookie and an external hard drive. Jake then transforms into a cheetah while making a fart noise. <a href="/wiki/Spirit_sparkles" title="Spirit sparkles">Sparkles</a> suddenly appear on the side of the house's roof. Finn and Jake try to squish them. The sparkles then lead them to a <a href="/wiki/Holo_technology" title="Holo technology">Holo-message player</a>. They turn it on to see that it is a message left from their father, <a href="/wiki/Joshua" title="Joshua">Joshua</a>, who built a <a href="/wiki/Joshua%27s_dungeon" title="Joshua's dungeon">dungeon</a> for them as babies. The <a href="/wiki/Kee-Oth" title="Kee-Oth">Demon</a> appears and demands Joshua to give his blood back. Joshua casts the demon away with a chant. Joshua secretly tells Jake that it is to toughen Finn up, while Finn covers his ears. Joshua explains how to get there, and also says that it will be really hard to finish the dungeon. They go to an unknown place where the <a href="/wiki/Dumb_Rock" title="Dumb Rock">Dumb Rock</a> was, as explained in the video. They move the rock out of the way of the entrance. When they enter they were attacked by a <a href="/wiki/Hot_Dog_Monster" title="Hot Dog Monster">Hot Dog Monster</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Hamburger_Monster" title="Hamburger Monster">Hamburger Monster</a>, after defeating them, they come to another prerecorded message where Joshua tells Jake to call Finn a whiny baby. They come across 2 paths, where one is a <a href="/wiki/Flower_Path" title="Flower Path">Flower Path</a> and a disgusting monster path. They take the flower path because Jake says that the monster is "super gross." The monster is saddened by this but Finn kindly reassures the monster, telling that they just said that because they are his bro's, and that bro's are real with each other. Finn adds "<i>You're gross, brother. Take a bath.</i>" to "amplify" their truthfulness.
</p><p>In the Flower Path, Finn asks Jake what their dad said. Jake tells that Joshua is surprised that a whiny baby like Finn made it that far. Finn is then puzzled; asking Jake why Joshua keeps saying things like that. Jake is forced to tell Finn that it is because Finn cries like a baby and that he is whiny. The <a href="/wiki/Fruit_Witch" title="Fruit Witch">Fruit Witches</a> then appear and try to get Finn to eat an apple, and Finn almost does, but Jake knocks the apple out of his hand and feeds it to one of the fruit witches. The cursed apple causes the Fruit Witch to have vines crawl out of her mouth and eyes, wrap around her, and turn into a giant apple. The two other Fruit Witches begin to eat the Fruit Witch, while Finn and Jake escape. The Fruit Witches begin to chase them up a stone wall, but Jake farts, knocking them out.
</p><p>Jake tells Finn what is really happening when they go into the final passage. They watch the tape as Joshua explains that he is finishing the last chamber of the dungeon. He explains that he is trying to fit a monster into a pit he dug. In the middle of what he is saying, two, long fingers with huge claws slowly sneak out of the entrance to the chamber and grab him. Joshua then gets pulled in the chamber, then Finn and Jake run into the room. While Finn attempts to get the sword, Jake discovers more Holo-Message tapes. He plays the final one informing him to play it for Finn when he finishes the dungeon. Behind Finn, two giant hands reach from the darkness, and Finn is slapped by the left hand. Finn is flown across the room, and the <a href="/wiki/Evil_Monster" title="Evil Monster">Evil Monster</a> crawls out of the darkness, revealing himself. When Finn expresses the doubt within himself because of their father's words, Jake says Joshua loves him and plays the tape to Finn revealing Joshua telling Finn he made the dungeon for him as well as expressing his pride for him. Having the confidence in himself restored, he rips the sword from its place and slays the monster. They leave the Dungeon with Joshua's sword when the demon appears. Jake replays the message where Joshua cast the demon away with the spell "<a href="/wiki/Kee_Oth_Rama_Pancake" title="Kee Oth Rama Pancake">Kee Oth Rama Pancake</a>." The episodes ends with the two saying their dad was "cool."
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episode_connections">Episode connections</span></h3>
<ul><li>The chant Joshua says to banish the demon is "<a href="/wiki/Kee_Oth_Rama_Pancake" title="Kee Oth Rama Pancake">Kee Oth Rama Pancake</a>." The <a href="/wiki/Wizard_Thief" title="Wizard Thief">Wizard Thief</a> in "<a href="/wiki/City_of_Thieves_(episode)" title="City of Thieves (episode)">City of Thieves</a>" also chants "Pancake" three times as an incantation to turn himself into a cat, suggesting "pancake" may be a magic word. It is also a reference to <a href="/wiki/Natasha_Allegri" title="Natasha Allegri">Natasha Allegri</a>'s cat named Pancake.
</li><li>When Finn said he only cries when people die, in the episode "<a href="/wiki/The_New_Frontier" title="The New Frontier">The New Frontier</a>" when Jake is about to die in Glob World, Finn cried. This means Finn was saying the truth.
<ul><li>This is proven false however, as Finn cried over Princess Bubblegum in <a href="/wiki/Incendium" title="Incendium">Incendium</a>.
</li></ul>
</li><li>The Three Fruit Witches look identical to the three girls in the title card of "<a href="/wiki/Slumber_Party_Panic" title="Slumber Party Panic">Slumber Party Panic</a>."
</li><li>After Finn and Jake outrun the Fruit Witches, the title card music of "<a href="/wiki/The_Lich_(disambiguation)" title="The Lich (disambiguation)">The Lich</a>" plays.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Errors">Errors</span></h3>
<ul><li>When the Giant Monster slaps Finn away from the sword, the key on its wrist disappears and reappears in the next scene.
</li><li>When Finn says he is going to look around, he goes to the left; however, when Jake follows him, he is to the right. But when Jake looked for Finn, he first looked to the left tunnel. It is possible Finn went to the right tunnel offscreen.
</li><li>When Finn is at the cross paths, a vent is on the flower path, but then when Finn is talking about them the vent is on the "That Guy" path. Also, the rocks that form the flower path change.
</li><li>When Joshua does the spell, he looks on the demon on the holo viewer, but when Jake replayed it on the demon, Joshua faces the actual demon instead of the one in the holo viewer.
</li><li>While Jake was replaying "Joshua's Final Message Remix," at some parts the video rewinds even when Jake does not press the rewind button.
</li><li>When Finn and Jake are watching the first holo-message the candle on the chimney disappears and the design of the chimney changes.
</li><li>The frames hung on Jake's bed doors only show violet and green backgrounds when they should show Lady Rainicorn and Finn next to some unknown green creature and the frame hung on Finn's bed should show some kind of green circle with symbols inside.
</li><li>In the last holo-message their dad is in some room (most likely a room in their house growing up) when the ugly monster takes their dad into the final room with the sword, but in the dungeon it is a dungeon room with the hole in it instead of the room their dad was in.
</li><li>When they get out of the dungeon they pass their tree house.
</li><li>At the time they get out of the dungeon they walk towards the setting sun. In the beginning of the episode, however, their dad says the dungeon is to the west meaning the sun set in the north instead of the west like it should have.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_notes">Production notes</span></h3>
<ul><li>This episode was originally called "Dungeon," sharing its name with the season one <a href="/wiki/Dungeon_(episode)" title="Dungeon (episode)">episode</a>, but it was changed to be "Dad's Dungeon."<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>
</li><li>Originally, this episode was going to be story-boarded by Pen alone, but Adam Muto and Natasha Allegri helped out when he did not have enough time.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup>
</li><li>Originally, this episode was going to include Shel Silverstein's version of "<a rel="nofollow" class="external text exitstitial" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KZNZt5qVgvM">A Boy Named Sue</a>," but it fell by the wayside.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[3]</a></sup> Nonetheless, Joshua calls Finn "Sue" in one of the hologram recordings.
</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Storyline_analysis">Storyline analysis</span></h3>
<ul><li>The key to the lock around the sword was on the Evil Monster's wrist. Finn actually broke the lock instead of taking the key from defeating the monster.
</li><li>The reason Joshua made the dungeon was because baby Finn was too "soft," and cried when squeezed. Note his disapproval of baby Finn's whining in the first hologram, where he squeezes both Jake and Finn (Jake merely giggles).
</li><li>In the last hologram before the last chamber, Joshua's messages could only make sense if he knew ahead of time that Finn and Jake would have chosen the flower path. That or the other (gross) path would not lead them to the end of the chamber.
</li></ul>
